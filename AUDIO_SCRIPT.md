# Build Week Audio Script

Target spoken duration: approximately 2 minutes 50 seconds. Screen directions in brackets are not spoken.

## 0:00-0:24

[Show the application header and five workflow steps.]

This project is about trustworthy AI, demonstrated through cataloging. AI can draft structured professional records quickly, but hidden evidence, unsupported assumptions, and opaque revisions make that dangerous. Libraries are an ideal proving ground because decisions must follow evidence, standards, and human judgment.

## 0:24-0:52

[Select Live mode. Upload `build-week-demo-source.pdf` and prepare the source package.]

Human-Guided AI MARC Review begins with human-provided evidence. This original fictional PDF includes publication details, summary, and contents. The extractor cites the page supporting every value and fills a visible Resource Source Package.

[Open the evidence disclosure briefly.]

Nothing hidden is sent to the cataloging roles. The expert can inspect, correct, and confirm exactly what they will receive.

## 0:52-1:20

[Confirm the package and choose Create AI draft.]

The first GPT-5.6 call is the AI Creator. It produces a conservative structured draft from only that confirmed evidence. Deterministic code supplies MARC 21 encoding. Unsupported facts remain blank, and uncertain classification remains visibly proposed rather than presented as authoritative. This is a draft, not a final record.

[Point to the plain-language fields and MARC preview.]

## 1:20-1:59

[Choose Ask independent AI Reviewer.]

The Reviewer is independent. A separate GPT-5.6 call receives the confirmed evidence, the Creator draft, and deterministic findings. It reviews all twelve MARC areas, but it can only recommend changes; it cannot alter the record.

[Open one LC vocabulary check.]

For subject analysis, services consult Library of Congress authorities. The interface separates source support, authorized form, correct subdivision construction, and appropriate application, with direct links to LC evidence.

## 1:59-2:25

[Open the actionable recommendation and choose Edit. Apply one meaningful MARC correction.]

The recommendation is still not a decision. The cataloger can accept it, reject it, or edit the MARC field directly. Here, the human expert corrects the structured heading. Only that explicit decision changes the record.

## 2:25-2:43

[Show Final MARC record, then the audit summary.]

The final MARC record is produced under human supervision. The audit preserves the source, Creator draft, Reviewer findings, authority evidence, human decisions, mode, final output, and any fallback.

## 2:43-2:59

[Return to the workflow header.]

Working with Codex, I transformed an existing prototype into this tested workflow with separate prompts, strict schemas, MARC encoding, and authority checks. Cataloging is the proof of concept; the architecture applies wherever experts review structured professional records. The goal is not autonomy. It is visible evidence, independent review, and human authorization.
